# Paper #3 — Learning outcomes (empirical)

## Working title

> **Learning outcomes from Sophie-authored content in undergraduate
> astronomy: a comparative study at a Hispanic-Serving Institution**

## Target venues

- **Primary**: [Astronomy Education Research (AER)](https://aer.aas.org/) —
  AAS's astronomy education journal. Discipline-specific; right
  audience.
- **Alternate**: [Physical Review Physics Education Research (PRPER)](https://journals.aps.org/prper/) —
  highest-impact physics-ed venue; rigorous reviewers.
- **Stretch**: [Computers & Education](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/computers-and-education) —
  broader pedagogical-tech audience; higher general-impact factor.

PRPER is the highest-prestige option but expects rigorous methodology;
AER is more discipline-friendly and accepts a wider range of study
designs. The right choice depends on study quality and methodological
depth at submission time.

## Type

Empirical comparative study. The contribution is *data*: real student
outcomes from ASTR 201 cohorts using Sophie vs. comparable cohorts
using prior course materials, disaggregated by demographic factors at
an HSI.

## Timing

- **Pilot data collection**: Spring 2027 (ASTR 201 Sp27 semester).
- **Drafting**: Summer 2027.
- **Submission**: September 2027.
- **Decision**: Q1 2028.

## Why this is the highest-impact paper

Three reasons:

1. **Real data is hard to fake.** Reviewers can argue about
   methods/conceptual papers; they cannot argue with a randomized or
   matched comparison showing statistically significant outcome
   differences.
2. **HSI demographic disaggregation is a publishable signal in
   itself.** Few astronomy education studies disaggregate by
   first-gen, Hispanic, or transfer student status. Sophie's
   deployment at SDSU produces this naturally.
3. **Direct evidence for the CAREER mid-period report.** Paper #3
   submission in September 2027 lines up with a CAREER mid-period
   reporting opportunity. Even a preprint counts as evidence of
   productivity.

This is the paper that **carries the tenure case** if DBER counts.

## Abstract sketch (study design assumption)

> Interactive scientific textbook platforms increasingly leverage AI
> authoring, but the impact on student learning outcomes — and the
> equity implications of these tools — remains under-studied. We
> report a quasi-experimental comparative study of learning outcomes
> in an undergraduate introductory astronomy course (ASTR 201) at a
> Hispanic-Serving Institution. Two cohorts (N = ~150 each) studied
> identical course content delivered through two platforms: a prior
> Quarto-based textbook (control) and Sophie, an AI-coauthored
> interactive platform implementing structural human-in-the-loop
> authoring (treatment). Outcome measures included a validated
> astronomy concept inventory (Light & Spectroscopy Concept Inventory),
> a confidence-calibration assessment, and course-level grade
> performance. We disaggregate results by demographics
> (first-generation status, Hispanic/Latine self-identification,
> transfer/continuing enrollment) to assess equity of impact.
> [Results placeholder.] We discuss implications for AI-augmented
> educational platform design and for equity-aware deployment in HSI
> contexts.

## Study design (working draft)

| Element | Plan |
|---------|------|
| **Design** | Quasi-experimental, matched cohort (Fa26 control → Sp27 treatment) or split-cohort (if feasible) |
| **Setting** | ASTR 201 at SDSU (HSI) |
| **Cohort size** | ~150 per semester typical enrollment |
| **Control condition** | Prior Quarto textbook + lectures |
| **Treatment condition** | Sophie textbook + same lectures + interactive Sophie components |
| **Pretest** | Demographic survey + Light & Spectroscopy Concept Inventory + confidence-calibration baseline |
| **Posttest** | Same instruments + course-grade performance |
| **Disaggregation** | Hispanic/Latine, first-gen, transfer/continuing, gender, prior STEM courses |
| **Analysis** | Mixed-effects regression with demographic interactions; effect sizes (Cohen's d); confidence intervals |
| **IRB** | Required; submit Spring 2026 for Spring 2027 data collection |

## Risks

- **IRB delays.** Fall 2026 submission gives a buffer; further delays
  could push pilot to Fa27 instead of Sp27, pushing submission to
  Q1 2028.
- **Cohort matching weakness.** If Fa26 (control) and Sp27
  (treatment) cohorts differ significantly in pre-course measures,
  the matched-cohort design weakens. Mitigation: use Fa25 baseline
  data if available and treat ASTR 201 history as a covariate.
- **Effect size smaller than anticipated.** Sophie's pedagogical
  improvement over Quarto may not produce a statistically significant
  outcome difference in a single-semester comparison. Mitigation:
  preregister analyses to avoid p-hacking accusations; report effect
  sizes and confidence intervals; frame as equity-disaggregated
  analysis even if main-effect is nullable.
- **AER vs. PRPER decision postponed too long.** Submit to PRPER
  first if methodology is rigorous; otherwise AER. Don't switch
  venues mid-review.
