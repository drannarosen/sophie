# 2026 calendar

The executable timeline for 2026. Anna committed to a double-sprint
on 2026-05-13: Cottrell Scholar Award (July 1) and NSF CAREER (July
22) in the same summer, with fall 2026 follow-on actions.

## The compressed reality

- **10–11 weeks** of focused proposal work, May 13 → July 22.
- **~15–20 hr/week** on proposals during this window.
- **Sophie v1 development slows** during the proposal sprint. The
  [Phase 1 plan](../status/phase-1-plan.md) should expect 6–8 weeks of
  reduced velocity. This is the cost; it's paid intentionally.
- **Cottrell and CAREER share narrative material.** You are writing
  one research-program story twice, in two formats: 5 pages of
  prose for Cottrell, 15 pages of project description for CAREER.

## Outcome scenarios

How the decision tree looks in early 2027 depending on what funds:

### Scenario A — Cottrell funded, CAREER funded

The best case. \$120K + \$400K+ over 3–5 years. Anna becomes a Cottrell
Scholar (prestigious pre-tenure recognition) with a CAREER award
(arguably *the* most important pre-tenure grant). Tenure case is
substantially de-risked. Focus shifts to executing the funded plans
and producing the planned papers.

### Scenario B — Cottrell funded, CAREER not funded

Still strong. Cottrell alone is a major win. Plan CAREER resubmission
for July 2027 (pre-tenure window likely still open). Use Cottrell
funding to support a Sophie postdoc / RA in 2027.

### Scenario C — CAREER funded, Cottrell not funded

CAREER alone is enormous; Cottrell loss is a minor sting. Carry on.
Cottrell can be re-applied to in 2027 if eligibility window remains.

### Scenario D — Neither funded

Disappointing but recoverable. Both have feedback cycles; revise both
based on panel comments and resubmit in 2027. Sloan LOI in fall 2026
remains active regardless. SDSU bridge funding and AAS-EPD Mini-Grant
provide tactical wins. Sophie v1 launches on schedule regardless of
funding outcomes.

In **all four scenarios**, fall 2026 looks the same: ship Sophie v1,
submit Sloan LOI, apply for AAS-EPD + SDSU, begin Paper #1 drafting.
