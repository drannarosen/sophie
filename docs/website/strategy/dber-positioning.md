# DBER positioning

The single highest-leverage open question in Anna's pre-tenure
strategy: **does SDSU astronomy count DBER (discipline-based education
research) and software-infrastructure work toward tenure?**

The answer determines whether Sophie can be the *centerpiece* of the
tenure case or must coexist with a parallel traditional-astrophysics
publication track. As of 2026-05-13, the answer is unknown — Anna has
not yet had this explicit conversation with her chair or tenure
mentor.

This page is the playbook for resolving the question, with minimal
political cost and maximum strategic clarity.

## Why this matters more than any single grant

If DBER counts in SDSU astronomy:

- Sophie is the tenure case. Three papers about Sophie + Cottrell +
  CAREER = a strong file.
- Time spent on Sophie is the highest-EV use of Anna's pre-tenure
  hours.

If DBER does not count in SDSU astronomy:

- Sophie has to coexist with a separate traditional-astro publication
  track (e.g., 2–3 first-author astrophysics papers over the next
  2–3 years).
- Sophie hours must be more carefully rationed — every Sophie hour
  competes with traditional-research hours.
- Grants still count regardless (NSF/NASA funding is binary-safe), so
  the funding strategy stands. But the paper strategy bifurcates.

The cost of *not knowing* is high: every week without an answer is a
week of allocating time without the constraint information that should
shape it.

## Dominant strategy under uncertainty

While the answer is unresolved, structure Sophie's outputs so they are
valuable in *both* worlds:

| Output type | Counts if DBER yes | Counts if DBER no |
|-------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **NSF / NASA grants** (Cottrell, CAREER, Sloan, IUSE) | Yes (substantial) | Yes (substantial — any major external funding counts) |
| **JOSS / methods paper** about Sophie | Yes | Maybe (depends on dept culture) |
| **DBER paper** in PRPER / AER / Computers & Ed | Yes (substantial) | Marginal |
| **Astro paper using Sophie-produced data** (e.g., ASTR 201 student CCD photometry outcomes) | Yes | Yes — reads as an astronomy paper |
| **Sophie itself as deliverable** | Yes | No (counts as service / teaching innovation) |

**The portfolio:** Pursue grants aggressively (binary-safe). Mix DBER
papers with hybrid astro-using-Sophie papers (cover both bases). Don't
bet everything on either world's signals exclusively.

## Sequence of moves (next 6 weeks)

The order matters. Don't go to the chair cold; don't go without
having read the RTP; don't go without a memo in hand.

1. **This week** — Locate and read SDSU Astronomy + College of Sciences
   RTP documents, plus the CSU-wide RTP umbrella. Note language
   about scholarly products, external funding, teaching/research
   integration.
2. **Next 2 weeks** — Draft the 1-page research-program memo (template
   below).
3. **Mid-June** — Meet your *tenure mentor* first. Lower-stakes than
   chair. Frame as "shaping my research program for the tenure case;
   want to talk through fit," not "asking permission."
4. **Late June / early July** (after Cottrell submitted) — 20-minute
   meeting with chair, memo in hand. You are confirming alignment, not
   seeking approval.

## The 1-page research-program memo (template)

Adapt language; keep length to one page.

```text
RESEARCH PROGRAM MEMO — Anna Rosen
Department of Astronomy, San Diego State University
[Date]

PROGRAM TITLE
AI-Augmented Pedagogical Infrastructure for Undergraduate STEM Education

CORE QUESTION
How can structured human-in-the-loop AI authoring produce interactive
scientific textbook content that improves measurable learning
outcomes compared to existing OER and commercial alternatives?

3-YEAR CONTRIBUTIONS
1. Software infrastructure (Sophie) — open-source AGPL platform.
   4 packages, 40+ ADRs, deployed in ASTR 101, ASTR 201, COMP 521.
2. Empirical learning-outcomes research — comparative studies with
   disaggregated ASTR 201 cohorts (HSI population).
3. Pedagogy-of-AI-authoring — novel structural HITL model with
   reference architecture (pedagogy-index pattern, schema-driven
   validation, contributor model).

EXPECTED OUTPUTS (24 months)
- 2–3 peer-reviewed papers (PRPER, AER, Computers & Education, JOSS)
- 1+ funded grant (Cottrell 2026, Sloan 2026, NSF CAREER 2026)
- v1 launch of Sophie + 2 SDSU courses live (fall 2026)
- Conference presentations: AAS, AAPT, AAS-EPD

ALIGNMENT WITH DEPARTMENT / COLLEGE RTP
[Fill in based on RTP language — placeholder]

REQUEST
- Confirm program aligned with tenure expectations
- Guidance on relative weighting of DBER vs. traditional astrophysics
- Names of senior faculty who could review CAREER proposal drafts
```

The memo's job is to **frame** the conversation. The chair walks in
expecting "Anna wants to talk about her tenure case" and walks out
having seen a structured, fundable, productive research program with
specific outputs. The framing is half the work; the conversation
itself is mostly confirming.

## RTP-document questions to answer in writing

When reading the RTP, look for explicit language on each:

1. Is "scholarly product" defined narrowly (refereed papers only) or
   broadly (software, datasets, OER, technical reports)?
2. What publication count is expected for tenure? Is first-authorship
   weighted differently than co-authorship?
3. Is external funding required, recommended, or merely "considered"?
   Are specific dollar levels referenced?
4. Is teaching evaluated separately from research, or can teaching
   innovation count under research as DBER?
5. Are there department or college statements about education research
   that name DBER, PER, or AER journals as acceptable?
6. How does the AAS / NASA SciAct / NSF-IUSE framing of "education
   research as scientific research" land in the dept's culture? (Often
   this isn't in the RTP; ask the mentor.)

## Indirect signals to look at first

Before the conversations, examine recent SDSU astronomy tenure
outcomes:

- Look at the last 3–5 SDSU astronomy faculty who got tenure.
  What did their CVs look like? How many papers? What types?
- Were any DBER-flavored?
- Did anyone with software-heavy contributions get tenure? With
  difficulty?
- The CSU-wide pattern across other astronomy departments (San José
  State, Cal Poly SLO, Fresno State, Sacramento State) — does CSU
  astronomy as a system value education work?

You may already know the answers anecdotally. Write them down; they
sharpen the memo.

## Risk: the chair conversation goes badly

Two failure modes to prepare for:

1. **"DBER doesn't count here."** This is bad news, but knowing it is
   strictly better than not knowing it. Plan immediately to begin a
   parallel traditional-astro paper track in summer 2026 (one paper,
   2nd-author or coauthor, leveraging existing data sets or
   collaborations). Sophie continues — it's still a Cottrell- and
   CAREER-fundable program — but its outputs reframe as teaching
   innovation, not research.
2. **"This isn't focused enough."** If the chair reads Sophie as
   defocused or peripheral, the memo isn't doing its job. Revise to
   sharpen the *research question* (the comparative learning-outcomes
   study or the AI-authoring methods contribution) rather than
   leading with the platform.

In either case, the memo and the mentor conversation should de-risk
the chair conversation. Don't go in blind.
