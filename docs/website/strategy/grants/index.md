## Strategic notes (shared across proposals)

These apply to *every* Sophie-adjacent proposal. Mention each
appropriately:

### HSI + CSU framing as structural Broader Impacts

SDSU is a Hispanic-Serving Institution and part of the CSU system
(mission-oriented toward teaching and serving underrepresented student
populations). This is a structural Broader Impacts win across CAREER,
IUSE, Cottrell, and SciAct. Disaggregated ASTR 201 demographics
should appear in every proposal's Broader Impacts section.

### R2 teaching load requires explicit research deliverables

Anna teaches 3 courses (ASTR 101 + ASTR 201 + COMP 521) at an R2.
Every Sophie-adjacent proposal needs a research deliverable distinct
from "build the platform" — typically a measurement study or
methods-paper contribution. Pair Sophie work with ≥1 astrophysics
paper per year to keep the research narrative live alongside the
infrastructure narrative.

### 40+ ADRs are a proposal asset

Sophie's [decision records](../../decisions/template.md) are not just
engineering hygiene. Cite them in sustainability sections and data
management plans as evidence of disciplined open-source development.
Reviewers respond well to projects with visible governance.

### AGPL one-liner for skeptical reviewers

> AGPL-3.0 ensures Sophie cannot be enclosed as proprietary IP by any
> contributor or downstream user; the platform remains as freely
> modifiable for educators in 20 years as it is today.

See [AGPL rationale](../agpl-rationale.md) for the full justification.

## Outcome dependencies

The grants pipeline assumes specific things land:

- **Sophie v1 (fall 2026)** must launch for the Sloan LOI (Nov 2026)
  to land. If v1 slips past December 2026, push Sloan to 2027 instead.
- **DBER stance from the chair** (see [DBER positioning](../dber-positioning.md))
  shapes how aggressively to lean into education-research framing in
  the CAREER proposal. Resolve this before the CAREER drafting cycle
  in late June 2026.
- **AAS-EPD Mini-Grant** call posting timing controls whether it lands
  in 2026 or 2027. Watch the AAS-EPD newsletter.
