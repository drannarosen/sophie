# Sloan Technology Grant — 2026

The Alfred P. Sloan Foundation's
**[Technology — Exploratory Grantmaking program](https://sloan.org/programs/digital-technology/exploratory-grantmaking-in-technology)**
is the best foundation fit for Sophie. Sloan has funded comparable
AGPL scholarly-publishing infrastructure projects (MyST,
ExecutableBooks, Jupyter, Pangeo). The funding shape — \$300K–\$750K
typical, rolling LOI-then-full-proposal — aligns with Sophie's needs.

**Submit LOI in November 2026, post-Sophie-v1 launch.**

## Program quick facts

- **Funder**: Alfred P. Sloan Foundation
- **Program**: Technology — Exploratory Grantmaking
- **Award**: Typically \$300K–\$750K, 1–3 year duration
- **Submission**: Rolling LOI; selected LOIs invited to full proposal
- **Decision timeline**: ~4–6 months LOI → decision; full proposal
  decision in subsequent ~4–6 months
- **Single PI**: Yes
- **License**: AGPL is acceptable (Sloan has funded copyleft scholarly
  infrastructure)

## Why Sloan

Sloan's Digital Technology program is one of very few foundations
that systematically funds *scholarly communication infrastructure*.
The funded portfolio includes:

- **MyST / ExecutableBooks** — the closest comparable to Sophie's
  rendering infrastructure layer.
- **Jupyter Project** — interactive scientific computing.
- **Pangeo** — open scientific computing infrastructure.

Sophie's pitch to Sloan: structural HITL AI-authoring as the
next-generation layer on top of MyST-style scholarly publishing
infrastructure. Adjacent to but distinct from existing portfolio.

## Why post-v1 launch

A foundation Technology LOI is most compelling when the project has
a working v1 to demonstrate. Pre-v1, Sophie reads as an architecture
proposal; post-v1, Sophie reads as a working platform with
demonstrated adoption (2 SDSU courses) that needs sustainability
support.

Specifically, the Sloan LOI benefits from:

- **A working public demo** — `drannarosen.github.io/astr201/` and
  `drannarosen.github.io/comp521/` live.
- **Real user adoption numbers** — even just Anna's two courses
  represent ~150–250 students using Sophie-authored content.
- **Public ADRs** — Sloan reviewers respond well to disciplined
  open-source governance.
- **Initial paper draft** — Paper #1 (JOSS methods paper) in progress
  by November 2026 signals research productivity.

## LOI structure (typical Sloan format)

Sloan LOIs are short — typically 2–3 pages — and serve to filter the
funnel. The format:

1. **Project summary** (~1 paragraph): what Sophie is, who it serves.
2. **Problem and opportunity** (~1 page): why structural HITL
   AI-authoring is a needed contribution to scholarly publishing
   infrastructure. Cite the [why-now narrative](../why-now.md).
3. **Approach** (~1 page): the technical architecture in brief; cite
   the [architecture explanation](../../explanation/architecture.md);
   the AGPL + ADR-driven governance.
4. **Funding ask + use of funds**: \$300K–\$500K over 2 years; specific
   line items (1 RA + 1 postdoc + infra + travel).
5. **Sustainability**: post-funding plan. Sophie remains AGPL OSS;
   community governance; Sophie Cloud as a separate optional
   sustaining mechanism (post-tenure).

## Funding ask shape

Approximately:

- \$300K–\$500K over 2 years
- Year 1: hire a postdoc for 12 months (\$85K + fringe ≈ \$115K) +
  Sophie infrastructure + travel
- Year 2: continue postdoc + RA support + community-building (Sophie
  workshops, conference presentations)

This is post-CAREER funding (if CAREER funds, this is supplementary;
if CAREER doesn't fund, this is core support). The Sloan funds
specifically enable *community-building* work — onboarding adopting
instructors, running workshops, expanding past Anna's own courses.

## Differentiator from CAREER

If both Sloan and CAREER fund, the activities don't overlap:

| Source | Funds |
|--------|-------|
| **CAREER** | Anna's research program: 5-year integrated research + education plan with measurable learning outcomes |
| **Sloan** | Sophie as community infrastructure: postdoc-led adoption, workshops, contributor cultivation, governance maturation |

CAREER funds the research; Sloan funds the platform community. No
budget overlap concerns.

## Contact + cultivation

Sloan's Technology program officer cultivation is helpful but not
required. Recommended:

- **Reach out informally** to Joshua Greenberg (Director, Digital
  Technology) or current program officer ~September 2026 with a
  short note: "We are an AGPL OSS scholarly-communications
  infrastructure project; would Sloan Technology be a fit?"
- **Attend Sloan-funded community events** if any align (MyST
  community calls, Jupyter community calls).

## Pitfalls

- **Don't pitch Sophie as a textbook.** Sloan funds *infrastructure*,
  not content. The platform is what's funded; the textbooks are
  byproducts.
- **Don't conflate with CAREER.** Different scope, different
  audience. Sloan reviewers are foundation program staff, not NSF
  panel reviewers.
- **Don't underweight sustainability.** Sloan asks "what happens
  after our money runs out?" If the answer is "Anna's salary,"
  that's weak. The answer should be: Sophie Cloud (post-tenure
  commercial option) + community contributors + university adoption.
