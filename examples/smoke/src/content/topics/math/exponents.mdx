<SkillReview.Card id="power-laws">
  <SkillReview.Prompt>
    What does $b^m \cdot b^n$ equal?
  </SkillReview.Prompt>
  <SkillReview.Answer>
    $b^{m+n}$ — multiplying same-base powers adds the exponents.
    This is the foundation rule from which the logarithm
    product-rule falls out.
  </SkillReview.Answer>
</SkillReview.Card>

<SkillReview.Card id="scientific-notation">
  <SkillReview.Prompt>
    Quick check before we keep going: what is $10^3$ in scientific notation, and what does the exponent tell you about the number's order of magnitude?
  </SkillReview.Prompt>
  <SkillReview.Answer>
    $10^3 = 1000$. The exponent (3) is the number's order of magnitude — how many factors of 10 above 1 it sits. Astronomy lives in scientific notation because distances span ${\sim}10^{27}$ orders.
  </SkillReview.Answer>
</SkillReview.Card>
