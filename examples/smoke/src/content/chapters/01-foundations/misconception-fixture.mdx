This chapter is a **smoke fixture** for the Intervention PR-γ extractor. It
pairs one misconception Aside with two literature-grounded interventions so
the pedagogy-index `interventions` collection populates from real MDX and

## Misconception with paired interventions

<Aside
  kind="misconception"
  name="universe-with-a-center"
  short="The universe expanded from a single center point — like a firework."
>
  Many students model the universe as expanding from a single center point —
  like a firework or explosion happening in a pre-existing space. The actual
  observation is isotropic Hubble flow from every vantage point; no center
  exists.

  <Intervention type="contrasting-cases" addresses="this">
    Predict what you'd observe if the universe had a center, then compare to
    the actual observation: isotropic Hubble flow from every vantage point.
    The contrast surfaces why "no center" follows from the data.
  </Intervention>

  <Intervention
    type="bridging-analogy"
    addresses="this"
    limits="Bread has an outside; the universe doesn't."
    depth="substantial"
  >
    Bread baking with raisins: from any raisin's perspective, every other
    raisin recedes — no raisin is "the center." The expansion is happening
    everywhere at once, not from a privileged point.
  </Intervention>
</Aside>

## Standalone (course-level) intervention

A second misconception is addressed by a refutation-text intervention
declared standalone (no enclosing Aside) — the kind of pattern an
instructor uses at module-end to consolidate the correct conception
across the chapter's spread.

<Intervention
  type="refutation-text"
  addresses="brighter-equals-closer"
>
  It's tempting to say a brighter star must be closer. But two stars at the
  same distance can differ in apparent brightness by orders of magnitude if
  one is intrinsically luminous (a hot supergiant) and the other is a dim
  red dwarf. Apparent brightness is a product of distance AND intrinsic
  luminosity — distance alone is not enough.
</Intervention>
