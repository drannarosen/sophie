The astronomical sky is a sphere. Coordinates locate a star on that
sphere; magnitudes describe how much of its light reaches us; angular
sizes encode resolution. Each measurement is a *number with a unit*,
and each unit will, eventually, demand a careful answer to *which
reference frame*, *whose telescope*, and *what wavelength range*.

## Apparent magnitude

The most basic observable for any star is how bright it appears. Bind
the concept across its representations before the real chapter unpacks
it:
