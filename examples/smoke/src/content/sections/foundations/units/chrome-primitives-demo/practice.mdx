# Practice problems

<PracticeProblem course="smoke" unit="chrome-primitives-demo" id="halpha-wavelength">
  <PracticeProblem.Prompt>
    What is the wavelength of the H$\alpha$ line, and what atomic
    transition does it correspond to?
  </PracticeProblem.Prompt>

  <Hint number={1}>H$\alpha$ is part of the Balmer series.</Hint>
  <Hint number={2}>The Balmer series transitions all end at $n=2$.</Hint>

  <Solution>
    H$\alpha$ has wavelength $\lambda = 656.3$ nm and corresponds to
    the $n=3 \to n=2$ electronic transition in atomic hydrogen.
  </Solution>
</PracticeProblem>

<PracticeProblem course="smoke" unit="chrome-primitives-demo" id="enclosed-mass">
  <PracticeProblem.Prompt>
    From the virial relation $M(r) = rv^2 / G$, find the radius $r$
    when $v = 220$ km/s and $M = 10^{11}\,M_\odot$.
  </PracticeProblem.Prompt>

  <Hint number={1}>Solve algebraically for $r$ first; then convert to CGS.</Hint>

  <Solution>
    $r = GM/v^2 \approx 9.4$ kpc — a typical galactic radius.
  </Solution>
</PracticeProblem>
