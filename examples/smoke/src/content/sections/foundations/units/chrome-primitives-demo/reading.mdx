# Chrome primitives demo

A short tour of Sophie's four chrome primitives. They are layout
containers — they hold pedagogical content; they are not pedagogy.

## A single Card

<Card title="Spectral classification">
  Stars are sorted from hottest (O-type, blue) through coolest
  (M-type, red) by surface temperature. The Sun is a G2V star at
  roughly 5,800 K.

  <Card.Footer>
    Source: Morgan-Keenan classification scheme (1943).
  </Card.Footer>
</Card>

## A Grid of three Cards

<Grid cols={3}>
  <Card title="O type">
    Hottest main-sequence stars; surface ~30,000 K. Blue light
    dominates the visible continuum.
  </Card>
  <Card title="G type">
    Sun-like stars; surface ~5,800 K. Yellow-white visible peak.
  </Card>
  <Card title="M type">
    Coolest main-sequence; surface ~3,500 K. Red, with strong
    molecular lines (TiO, VO).
  </Card>
</Grid>

## Tabs across representational modes

<Tabs defaultLabel="Continuum">
  <Tab label="Continuum">
    A blackbody continuum encodes effective temperature via Wien's
    displacement law: λ_peak × T = 2.898 × 10⁻¹ cm·K (CGS).
  </Tab>
  <Tab label="Line spectra">
    Absorption and emission lines arise from quantized atomic
    transitions. Each element has a fingerprint of wavelengths.
  </Tab>
  <Tab label="Composition">
    Line strength encodes the relative abundance of each element in
    the stellar photosphere — the breakthrough Cecilia Payne
    formalized in 1925.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Dropdown — single-item shorthand

<Dropdown
  client:load
  course="smoke"
  unit="chrome-primitives-demo"
  id="deep-dive-hydrogen"
  label="Deep Dive: Hydrogen's atomic fingerprint"
>
  The red glow of an H II region comes from hydrogen's H-alpha
  transition at 656.3 nm — an electron dropping from the n=3 to n=2
  level. Each Balmer line is a different jump down to n=2.
</Dropdown>

## Dropdown — multi-item accordion

<Dropdown
  client:load

  id="blackbody-tour"
>
  <Dropdown.Item label="Wien's displacement law">
    λ_peak T = 2.898 × 10⁻¹ cm·K (CGS). Hotter stars peak at shorter
    wavelengths.
  </Dropdown.Item>
  <Dropdown.Item label="Stefan-Boltzmann law">
    L = 4π R² σ T⁴. A star's luminosity scales with the fourth power
    of its surface temperature for a fixed radius.
  </Dropdown.Item>
</Dropdown>
